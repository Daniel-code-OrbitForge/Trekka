COOKIE POLICY

Last Updated: November 15, 2025

Trekka uses cookies and similar technologies to improve your experience on our platform. This policy explains what are, how we use them, and how you can control their use.

1. WHAT ARE COOKIES?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or phone) when you visit a website. They are help website function smoothly and remember useful information about your visit.


There are two main types of cookies:

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies:

  • Persistent cookies: Stay on your device until deleted or expired.
  • Session cookies: Disappear when you close your browser.

2. HOW WE USE COOKIES

Trekka uses cookies to make our platform work properly, improve performance, and personalize your experience.


ESSENTIAL COOKIES

These cookies keep the platform running. You can't disable them because they:

  • Keep you logged in securely.
  • Protect your account from fraud.
  • Manage load balancing for speed and stability.
  • Enable booking and checkout functions.

FUNCTIONALITY COOKIES

These cookies remember your preferences and help personalize your experience. They:

  • Remember your language and currency settings.
  • Recall your search history and recent trips.
  • Save your preferred pickup or destination.

ANALYTICS COOKIES

We use these cookies to understand how users interact with Trekka. They:

  • Track which pages are most visited.
  • Measure performance and load times.
  • Identify bugs and usability issues.
  • Help us improve navigation and features.

We use tools like Google Analytics for this purpose.


MARKETING COOKIES

These cookies help us deliver relevant ads and measure campaign effectiveness. They:

  • Show you personalized promotions.
  • Limit how often you see an ad.
  • Track ad clicks and conversions.
  • Improve how ads are targeted on other websites.

3. THIRD-PARTY COOKIES

Some cookies come from trusted partners we work with:

Payment processors:(e.g; Paystack, Flutterwave) for secure transactions.

Social Media:(e.g; Facebook, Twitter/(X), Instagram, LinkedIn) for sharing and integration.

Analytics Provider:(e.g; Google analytics) to track performance.

Advertising Networks:(e.g; Google Ads, Facebook Ads, Facebook Pixel) to deliver targeted ads.


4. OTHER TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

We may also use:

Web Beacons (Pixels): Small images used to track page visits or email opens.

Local Storage: Saves larger data directly in your browser.

Session Storage: Temporarily stores data while you browse.


5. MANAGING YOUR COOKIE PREFERENCES

You have full control over how cookies are used.

BROWSER SETTINGS

You can manage cookies directly from your browser by choosing to:

  • View or delete stored cookies.
  • Block third-party cookies.
  • Block cookies from specific websites.
  • Delete all cookies when closing your browser.

COOKIE PREFERENCE TOOL

Use our Cookie Settings link (found at the bottom of every page) to:

  • Accept or reject certain cookie types.
  • Review cookie details.
  • Update your preferences any time.

OPT-OUT OPTIONS

You can opt out specific tracking services:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Google Ad Settings
  • Facebook Ad Preferences

6. IMPACT OF DISABLING COOKIES

If you disable cookies, some features may not work as intended:

  • You may be logged out automatically.
  • The booking process may fail to complete.
  • Your preferences may not be saved.
  • Pages may load slower.

Essential cookies cannot be disabled because they're required for the platform to function.


7. DO NOT TRACK (DNT) SIGNALS

Some browsers allow you to send "Do Not Track" signals. Currently, there's no universal standard for responding to DNT signals, so Trkka does not respond to them at this time.


8. MOBILE DEVICES

Mobile devices use unique identifiers similar to cookies:

  • iOS: Identifier for Advertisers (IDFA)
  • Android: Google Advertising (GAID)

You can limit ad tracking or reset these identifiers in your device's settings.
